The history of John Adams

This paragraph will be based on John Adams

John Adams (October 30 [O.S. October 19] 1735 – July 4, 1826) was an American statesman and Founding Father who served as the first Vice President (1789–1797) and second President of the United States (1797–1801). He was a lawyer, diplomat, political theorist, and leader of the movement for American independence from Great Britain. He was a dedicated diarist and his correspondence, including with his wife and closest advisor, Abigail Smith Adams, provides important historical information on the era.
